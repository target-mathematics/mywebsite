Midpoint Formula

If the midpoint between two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is $(x, y)$, then

$(x, y)= \left(\dfrac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \dfrac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right) $

Distance Formula

If the distace between two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is

$\text{distance}=\sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - 1_1)^2}$

Example (1)

If the coordinates of the two points $P$ and $Q$ are $(3,-4)$ and $(5,4)$ respectively. Find:

(a) the midpoint of $PQ$.

(b) the length of segment $PQ$.

Solution

$P=(3,-4), Q=(5,4)$

midpoint of $PQ$

$\begin{aligned} &=\left(\dfrac{3+5}{2}, \dfrac{-4+4}{2}\right)\\\\ &=(4,0) \end{aligned}$

length of $P Q$

$\begin{aligned} &=\sqrt{(5-3)^{2}+(4+4)^{2}} \\\\ &=\sqrt{4+64} \\\\ &=\sqrt{68} \end{aligned}$
